# Commercial Licensing

Linux Made Sane Community Edition is free for normal internal use, including internal use by commercial organisations.

A commercial agreement from Richard D. Kiernan is required when LMS becomes part of a third-party commercial service, product, or customer-delivery model.

## Commercial License Required

- MSP usage where LMS manages infrastructure belonging to third-party customers
- hosted LMS offered to third parties
- SaaS or managed service usage
- white-label resale
- embedding LMS in another commercial platform
- redistribution as part of a paid appliance, image, or package
- use of Pro, Business, or Enterprise-only modules

## Commercial License Not Required

- a company using LMS internally to manage its own Linux servers
- a developer using LMS on their own machines
- a homelab
- schools or universities using LMS internally
- charities or non-profits using LMS internally
- consultants using LMS only to manage their own internal infrastructure

If your use is close to the boundary, ask Richard D. Kiernan before building a customer-facing service around LMS.
